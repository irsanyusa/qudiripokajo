    Whether you are looking to lose weight, stay fit, or simply acquire healthier eating habits, getting information about the caloric values of the foods you eat is the key to starting a healthy lifestyle. Also, keep in mind that appearances can be very deceiving. A seemingly harmless small portion of certain foods can be true calorie bombs, while others can be eaten in large amounts with pretty much no guilt.

    As good fitness freaks, we at Bright Side managed to do the math to see what it means to ingest 1,000 calories of some pretty popular foods. Diet starts Monday, we mean it this time.

    1. Cheeseburger

    1,000 cal = 14 oz (396 g)
    Portion: 2 + 1/2 burgers

    2. Hotdog

    1,000 cal = 13.4 oz (379 g)
    Portion: 3 + 1/3 hotdogs

    3. Sandwich

    1,000 cal = 12 oz (340 g)
    Portion: 4 sandwiches

    4. Shawarma

    1,000 cal = 17 oz (481 g)
    Portion: 1 + 1/3

    5. Spaghetti

    1,000 cal = 27 oz (1,048 g)
    Portion: 3 + 1/2 plates

    6. Sushi

    1,000 cal = 24 oz (680 g)
    Portion: 26 pieces

    7. Eggs (Scrambled)

    1,000 cal = 37 oz (1,048 g)
    Portion: 4 + 1/4 plates (4 eggs per plate)

    8. Grilled Fish

    1,000 cal = 26 oz (737 g)
    Portion: 6 fish

    9. Cheesecake

    1,000 cal = 11 oz (309 g)
    Portion: 5 slices

    10. Cookies

    1,000 cal = 7.5 oz (212 g)
    Portion: 13 cookies

    11. Chocolate

    1,000 cal = 7 oz (198 g)
    Portion: 4 + 1/2 bars

    12. Potatoes

    1,000 cal = 45 oz (1,275 g)
    Portion: 6 potatoes

    13. Cucumbers

    1,000 cal = 250 oz (7,087 g)
    Portion: 17 + 1/2 cucumbers

    14. Lettuce

    1,000 cal = 250 oz (7,087 g)
    Portion: 12 heads

    15. Peas

    1,000 cal = 44 oz (1,247 g)
    Portion: 13 cups

    16. Bananas

    1,000 cal = 40 oz (1,133 g)
    Portion: 9 bananas

    17. Oranges

    1,000 cal = 77 oz (2,182 g)
    Portion: 16 + 1/2 oranges

    18. Watermelon

    1,000 cal = 125 oz (3,543 g)
    Portion: 12 wedges (3 watermelons)

    19. Avocado

    1,000 cal = 22 oz (623 g)
    Portion: 3 avocados

    20. Coffee

    1,000 cal = 3,990 fl oz (118 L)
    Portion: 500 cups

    21. Ice Tea

    1,000 cal = 125 fl oz (3.69 L)
    Portion: 10 1/2 glasses

    22. Milkshake

    1,000 cal = 30 fl oz (887 ml)
    Portion: 3 glasses

    23. Orange Juice

    1,000 cal = 71 fl oz (2 L)
    Portion: 8 + 1/2 glasses

    24. Cheese

    1,000 cal = 8.8 oz (250 g)
    Portion: 10 slices

    25. Ham

    1,000 cal = 25 oz (708.7 g)
    Portion: 27 slices

    26. Pretzel

    1,000 cal = 10.5 oz (297 g)
    Portion: 2 + 1/2 pretzels

    Do you have any nutritional information that might come in handy to help someone to start a healthier diet? Please share it with us in the comments.
